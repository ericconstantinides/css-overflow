Special Image CSS-Overflow classes

This text is inside of a body container. It's narrow, but perfect for reading. Although the text is perfect for reading, having dramatic images is key for enticing viewers. The following will show 3 different examples of images that overflow out of their containers using absolutely no JavaScript or breaking the HTML flow. On mobile, these images always overflow to the viewport width

.css-overflow--inner-body-width:

On wider viewports, css-overflow--inner-body-width will appear like any other image. However, when you shrink the width of the viewport down, the image will break out of the body-container and kiss the viewport sides.

.css-overflow--site-container-width:

css-overflow--site-container-width breaks out of the body-container and pushes out to the site-container.

.css-overflow--full-browser-width:

Lastly, css-overflow--full-browser-width overflows the whole time to the browsers width - really great for adding dramatic images to your site.
